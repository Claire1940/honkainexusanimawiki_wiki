<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Systems: Turn-Based vs Auto-Battler Strategy">

The most significant difference between the two games lies in their combat systems. *Honkai: Star Rail* relies on a classic turn-based formula where speed, skill points, and ultimate timings dictate success. *Honkai: Nexus Anima* pivots to a strategic auto-battler style where pre-battle preparation and positioning are the keys to victory.

In *Nexus Anima*, combat takes place in a pocket dimension called a Semi-plane. Players deploy their chosen Anima masters and companion creatures onto a grid. Once the battle starts, the creatures fight semi-autonomously based on their roles and Aspect traits, while the player supports them using master abilities.

### Combat Comparison

| Combat Element | Honkai: Nexus Anima | Honkai: Star Rail |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Control Style** | Pre-placement and real-time support | Direct turn-by-turn manual commands |
| **Team Size** | Multiple Anima companions + 1 Animaster | 4 playable characters |
| **Damage Types** | Aspect-based elemental synergies | Toughness break and elemental weaknesses |
| **Positioning** | Grid placement directly impacts targeting | Static line-up (frontline/backline is abstract) |

<Callout type="warning" title="Strategy Focus">
Do not expect to manually button-mash your way through *Honkai: Nexus Anima*. Your success depends entirely on how well you understand Anima placement, frontline defense, and elemental Aspect synergies before the round begins.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Creature Collection and Evolution Systems">

*Honkai: Nexus Anima* introduces a deep creature-collecting ecosystem that does not exist in *Honkai: Star Rail*. In *Star Rail*, progression is centered entirely on human characters acquired through the gacha system. In *Nexus Anima*, players explore the world to find, befriend, and battle wild Anima.

Anima creatures have distinct visual designs, personalities, and multi-stage evolution paths. For example, players can raise an Inkifoal, watch it evolve into an Inkicorn, and eventually reach its final form, Atragasus.

<Section icon={Target} title="Lore, Characters, and World Structure">

Both games are deeply rooted in the Honkai universe, but they approach their settings differently. *Honkai: Star Rail* features a space-faring narrative where the Trailblazer travels from planet to planet on a linear cosmic rail. *Honkai: Nexus Anima* is set in the "New World," which is divided into 81 distinct Planes after a cataclysmic event shattered the cosmic network of energy known as the Nexus.

Interestingly, *Nexus Anima* reuses and reimagines iconic characters from both *Honkai Star Rail* and *Honkai Impact 3rd*.
