<Section icon={Target} title="Core Mechanics of Nexus Battles">

To excel in Nexus Battles, you must master the fundamental rules of the arena board. Combat begins after you deploy your Anima units onto the grid. Once the round starts, units target enemies based on proximity, attack range, and specific behavior AI.

### Understanding Aspect Synergy
In TFT, you build around traits like Chrono or Portal. In Honkai: Nexus Anima, you build around **Aspects**—remnant conceptual powers left behind by broken universal bonds. These Aspects dictate how units interact, share buffs, and counter opposing elements.

### Board Placement Rules
Your board setup dictates the flow of battle. Putting fragile utility units in harm's way leads to swift defeat, whereas proper front-to-back layering ensures your damage dealers can execute their abilities.

| Unit Role | Ideal Board Position | Primary Objective | Example Unit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Frontline Protector** | Front row center | Absorb damage, draw aggro, create space | Fyrklor (Strength Aspect) |
| **Melee Striker** | Front row flank | Pressure enemy backline, deal physical damage | Scorscythe (War Aspect) |
| **Backline Support** | Back row corners | Provide healing, crowd control, or shields | Umpurrella (Featured Utility) |
| **Growth Anchor** | Middle row protected | Survive to scale up power over time | Inkifoal (Evolution Line) |

<Callout type="warning" title="Flanking Hazards">
Be mindful of high-mobility enemy strike units. If you leave your backline entirely unprotected, fast melee threats will bypass your frontline protector and eliminate your utility units instantly.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Step-by-Step Team Building Guide">

Building a competitive team in Honkai: Nexus Anima follows a structured process. Use these steps to transition from a basic starter setup to a highly synchronized, TFT-style composition.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Anchor Unit">
    Choose a primary frontline protector to serve as the anchor of your team. A unit like **Fyrklor** is ideal because its Strength Aspect provides high survivability, creating a safe zone for your backline.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Add Role Coverage">
    Introduce a secondary damage dealer or utility unit. If you need aggressive pressure, pair your anchor with **Scorscythe** (War Aspect). If you need defensive utility, opt for a companion like **Umpurrella**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Activate Aspect Synergies">
    Look at the concept bonds of your chosen units. Match paired concept Aspects (such as Love and Hate, or Strength and Satiation) to unlock team-wide combat bonuses that increase attack speed, defense, or energy generation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Position Your Grid">
    Place your protector in the front row, your melee strikers on the outer flanks, and your squishy utility or evolution units safely in the rear. Double-check that your backline is not exposed to direct paths.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Evolution Advantage">
When using progression-focused units like the **Inkifoal → Inkicorn → Atragasus** line, prioritize keeping them alive. These units grow exponentially stronger mid-battle as their evolution triggers, mimicking the power spike of hitting a 3-star unit in TFT.
